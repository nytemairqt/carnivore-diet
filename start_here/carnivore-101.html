Carnivore 101

A beginner's guide to the all-meat diet: what it is, why it works, and how to start.

What Is the Carnivore Diet?

The carnivore diet is exactly what it sounds like: you eat only animal products. Meat, fish, eggs, and (optionally) dairy. No plants, no grains, no fruits, no vegetables, no nuts, no seeds, no processed foods. Just meat, salt, and water.

Modern food is a science experiment. Highly processed seed oils, refined carbohydrates, chemical additives, and plant foods bred for yield and shelf life, not human health. The result: skyrocketing rates of autoimmune disease, digestive disorders, metabolic syndrome, and mental health issues. The carnivore diet basically strips all of that away and asks a simple question: what happens when you eat only the foods humans evolved on?

Why Are People Doing It?

People don't end up on carnivore because they're looking for a new way to eat. They end up here because everything else failed. The stories cluster around a few categories:

Gut issues. This is the #1 reason people try carnivore. IBS, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis, SIBO, chronic bloating, reflux - the list goes on. Plant foods contain fiber and defense chemicals (lectins, oxalates, phytates) that can irritate sensitive guts. Removing them completely gives the intestinal lining a chance to heal. The results are often dramatic - many people report normal digestion for the first time in their lives within days or weeks.

Skin conditions. Eczema, psoriasis, acne, keratosis pilaris, dermatitis - all inflammatory conditions that often clear up when plant foods are removed. The gut-skin axis is real: heal the gut, and the skin follows.

Autoimmune conditions. Rheumatoid arthritis, Hashimoto's, lupus, multiple sclerosis. Carnivore is being used as a therapeutic diet to reduce autoimmune burden. By removing dietary antigens (plant proteins that cross-react with human tissue), the immune system gets a break. Many people report reduced pain, lower inflammation markers, and fewer flares.

Hormones and periods. Women report more regular cycles, reduced PMS, lighter periods, and improved fertility on carnivore. The mechanism is multi-factorial: stable blood sugar supports hormonal regulation, adequate fat intake provides the building blocks for steroid hormones, and the absence of plant estrogens (phytoestrogens) may allow the endocrine system to reset.

Mental health. Stable blood sugar eliminates the mood swings, anxiety, and brain fog that come with carb-driven energy crashes. The gut-brain axis plays a role too - many people report reduced anxiety and depression within a few weeks. Ketosis and proper satiety may provide further cognitive benefit, helping to focus on difficult tasks without the distracting munchies.

Weight and metabolic health. Type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, obesity - carnivore removes carbohydrates entirely, which normalizes blood sugar and insulin. Weight loss happens naturally without calorie counting because the diet is highly satiating. Higher protein also provides the building blocks to repair and rebuild your muscles and connective tissue.

How Do I Get Started?

The starting template is simple:

Muscle meat + fat, salt, water. That's it. Eat fatty cuts of meat - ribeye, 80/20 ground beef, lamb chops, pork belly. Add salt to taste. Drink water when thirsty. Don't count calories. Eat until full.

For the first few weeks, don't worry about organ meats, dairy, or any of the advanced protocols. Just meat, salt, water. Get adapted first.

Key insight: Transition slowly. Don't go from the standard high-carb diet to carnivore overnight. Taper down carbs over 1-2 weeks to minimize the adaptation side effects (headaches, fatigue, irritability - collectively called the "keto flu" or "carnivore flu"). Alternatively, reduce carbs to ~50g/day for a week before going to zero.

Practical tips for the first week:

Buy cheaper cuts of meat if budget is a concern - chuck roast, oxtail, ground beef, chicken thighs with skin. Cook in animal fats (tallow, butter, lard), not seed oils. Salt your food to taste - your sodium needs increase when you stop eating carbs. Electrolyte supplements can help bridge the adaptation period.

What to expect: Days 2-5 are usually the hardest. You may feel tired, headachy, and crave carbs. This passes. By day 7-10, most people report waking up before their alarm, steady energy throughout the day, and dramaticaly reduced hunger.
