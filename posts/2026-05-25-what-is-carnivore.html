What Is the Carnivore Diet?

A clear overview of the all-meat diet: what to eat, why it works, and who it might help

May 25, 2026

New here? Start with this overview, then check out my 30-day experience for a real-world account.

The Short Version

The carnivore diet is an elimination diet consisting entirely of animal products: meat, fish, eggs, and sometimes dairy. No plants, no grains, no fruits, no vegetables, no nuts, no seeds, no processed foods. Just meat, salt, and water.

It's arguably the most restrictive elimination diet you can do - and that's exactly the point. By removing everything potentially problematic, you give your body a clean baseline, then reintroduce foods one at a time to identify what causes issues.

What You Eat

The food list is short. Really short:

• Beef - ribeye, sirloin, ground beef, brisket, liver (the king of meats)
• Other red meat - lamb, bison, goat, venison
• Poultry - chicken, turkey, duck (less nutrient-dense than red meat, fine in moderation)
• Pork - bacon, pork chops, pork belly (quality matters - look for pasture-raised)
• Fish and seafood - salmon, sardines, mackerel, cod, shrimp
• Eggs - ideally pasture-raised
• Animal fats - butter, tallow, lard, ghee
• Dairy - butter is universally tolerated; cheese and heavy cream are optional (some people react)

That's it. No plants, no carbs, no fiber. Just animal foods cooked simply - grilled, pan-seared, air-fried, sous vide, or raw.

The Rationale

The argument for carnivore rests on a few key scientific observations:

1. Plants contain defense chemicals. Plants can't run away, so they evolved chemical defenses - lectins, oxalates, phytates, tannins, goitrogens, and more. These compounds can irritate the human gut, block mineral absorption, and trigger inflammatory responses in sensitive individuals.

2. Fiber is not essential. The idea that humans need dietary fiber is surprisingly unsupported by evidence. Populations that thrive on zero-fiber diets (the Maasai, Inuit, traditional Mongolian herders) show no higher rates of constipation or colon disease. The "fiber prevents colon cancer" hypothesis failed in large randomized controlled trials.

3. Animal foods are the most nutrient-dense foods on the planet. Liver is richer in vitamins and minerals than any plant food. Red meat is the best source of bioavailable iron, zinc, B12, and complete protein. The nutrients in meat are more absorbable than the same nutrients in plants (which are often bound to anti-nutrients like phytates and oxalates).

4. Stable blood sugar. Zero carbohydrates means zero blood sugar spikes. This eliminates the energy crashes, cravings, and mood swings that come with a carb-based diet. Many people report dramatic improvements in mental clarity and mood stability.

Who It Helps Most

Based on community reports and emerging clinical experience, the people who seem to benefit most are:

• People with autoimmune conditions (rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, Hashimoto's)
• People with chronic digestive issues (IBS, IBD, SIBO, bloating, reflux)
• People with skin conditions (eczema, psoriasis, acne, keratosis pilaris)
• People with metabolic syndrome (type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, obesity)
• People with mental health issues (anxiety, depression, brain fog) - the gut-brain axis is real
• People who just want stable energy without afternoon crashes

Common Concerns

"Won't you get scurvy?" No. Meat contains plenty of vitamin C - especially fresh meat and organ meats. The requirement is lower on a low-carb diet because glucose competes with vitamin C for absorption.

"What about cholesterol / heart disease?" The lipid-heart hypothesis has been seriously weakened in recent years. Dietary cholesterol has minimal impact on blood cholesterol for most people. Many carnivores see improved lipid profiles (lower triglycerides, higher HDL, lower LDL-P).

"Isn't red meat bad for you?" The epidemiology linking red meat to disease is based on food frequency questionnaires (notoriously unreliable) and fails to separate processed from unprocessed meat. When you look at actual randomized controlled trials, red meat is neutral or beneficial for most health markers.

"What about the environment?" Regenerative grazing actually builds soil, sequesters carbon, and supports biodiversity. Feedlot beef is a different story - which is why sourcing from regenerative farms (like ButcherBox) matters.

Getting Started

If you want to try it, the approach is simple:

1. Eat meat, drink water. That's the whole protocol for the first 30 days.
2. Salt your food generously. The adaptation phase depletes sodium fast.
3. Consider electrolyte supplementation for the first week or two.
4. Eat fatty cuts of meat - lean meat + insufficient fat = "rabbit starvation" (nausea, weakness).
5. Don't count calories. Eat until full. Your appetite will self-regulate within days.
6. Give it at least 30 days before judging results. The first week can be rough.

For a deeper dive, Shawn Baker's The Carnivore Diet and Paul Saladino's The Carnivore Code are excellent resources.
