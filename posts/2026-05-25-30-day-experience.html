My 30-Day Carnivore Diet Experience

Gut health, skin, energy - what changed in one month on an all-meat diet

May 25, 2026

Disclaimer: This is a personal experience report, not medical advice. Diet changes affect everyone differently. Consult your doctor before starting any new diet.

Thirty days ago I started the carnivore diet - meat, salt, water. Nothing else. No plants, no carbs, no processed food. I had been dealing with gut issues, skin problems, and low energy for years. Conventional advice (more fiber, more veggies, "balanced" diet) never really fixed it. I decided to try the elimination diet to end all elimination diets.

Here's what happened.

Week 1 - The Adaptation Phase (aka the flu)

Days 2 through 5 were rough. Headaches, fatigue, weird sleep. This is the "keto flu" / "carnivore flu" phase where your body is switching from burning glucose to burning fat for fuel. Your electrolyte balance shifts dramatically because you're no longer holding onto water the same way.

What got me through it: LMNT electrolyte salts in my water and extra salt on everything. By day 6 the fog lifted. I woke up before my alarm for the first time in years.

Week 2 - Gut Changes (Dramatic)

This is where things got interesting. My lifelong bloating dissapeared. Completely. No gas, no cramps, no afternoon "food coma." I used to feel bloated after pretty much every meal regardless of what I ate. On carnivore - nothing. Just quiet digestion.

The mechanism makes sense: plant foods contain defense chemicals (lectins, oxalates, phytates, tannins) that irritate the gut lining. Remove them all and your gut gets a chance to heal. No fiber needed - your body doesn't actually require dietary fiber.

Week 3 - Skin Clearing

I've had mild but persistent skin issues for as long as I can remember. Small bumps on my arms (keratosis pilaris), occasional acne, general redness. By week three, my skin was clearer than it's been since I was a kid. The bumps on my arms smoothed out. No new breakouts.

This tracks with the elimination diet logic: dairy and plants are two of the most common dietary triggers for skin inflammation. Many people on carnivore eventually reintroduce dairy to test tolerance. I'm staying strict for now.

Week 4 - Energy and Mental Clarity

The most noticeable change by week four: stable energy all day. No post-meal crashes, no needing caffeine to get through the afternoon. My mental clarity improved significantly - sharper focus, less brain fog, better mood stability.

I attribute this to the absence of blood sugar swings. Without carbohydrates, your blood glucose stays remarkably stable. Your brain runs on ketones, which provide a steadier fuel supply than glucose.

What I Eat in a Day

Simple template that requires almost zero effort:

Breakfast: Skip it (not hungry - the stable blood sugar kills cravings)
Lunch: 8oz ribeye or sirloin, cooked in butter or tallow, salted heavily
Dinner: 8-12oz ground beef (80/20) with eggs fried in beef tallow
Snack (if needed): Beef jerky or hard-boiled eggs
Drinks: Water with LMNT electrolytes

The key insight: eat when hungry, stop when full. No counting calories or macros. The appetite regulation on carnivore is remarkable - you naturally eat the right amount.

What's Next

I'm staying on carnivore for at least 90 days total before considering reintroductions. My plan is to slowly test tolerance for eggs (if going beyond beef-only), then dairy (butter is already fine), then select low-toxin plants.
